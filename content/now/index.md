A snapshot of what I am focused on now, in the spirit of the [now page](https://nownownow.com/about). Updated when priorities shift.

### Teaching and curriculum

I direct the Master's in Health Informatics at FMUP and am helping shape training in digital health and biomedical innovation so students leave with practical competence, not just theoretical familiarity. This month I have been running short hands-on sessions for PhD students on the tools that usually are not taught — terminal use, version control, reference management, and [AI coding agents](/notes/opencode/) — with live walkthroughs rather than slides. I want students to know how to evaluate systems, design better workflows, and communicate clearly about evidence.

### AI evaluation in healthcare

I am focused on the unglamorous-but-critical part of AI in medicine: what happens after prototype enthusiasm. External validation, reporting quality, human factors, safety, and implementation constraints are where most projects either become useful or quietly fail. The reading list I teach from is maintained as a [separate note](/notes/emhi/); the philosophical stance behind it is in [Implementing AI in health](/essays/ai-health/).

### Writing in public

I have just finished a major overhaul of [the scientific writing note](/notes/sw/) — now a full practical guide for doctoral students and early-career researchers, with worked examples, figures, and references to the *Breathe* *Doing Science* series. The [OpenCode guide](/notes/opencode/) has also been rebuilt around a simple idea: a tool that stays local and legible is more useful to a health researcher than a faster but opaque one. I publish here, under version control, so practical questions about reproducibility, reference management, and using AI tools without giving up rigour have durable answers I can point students at.

## Recently

In the first half of April I gave two keynotes in northern Portugal: on [health data literacy and interprofessional teams](/keynotes/2026-health-data-literacy/) in Oliveira de Azeméis, and on [AI in health through Postman's lens](/keynotes/2026-ia-saude-bienal-valongo/) in Valongo. Both touched the same question in different registers: what do these technologies undo while they add?
