I have been teaching at the Faculty of Medicine of the University of Porto (FMUP) since 2008, joining the tenure track as Assistant Professor on 22 December 2025 through the FCT-Tenure programme. Most of my teaching sits at the boundary between ideas and implementation: electronic health records, productivity systems for researchers, digital therapeutics, AI evaluation, and scientific writing. My goal is practical fluency: students leaving a course able to do something, not just define something.

I also co-direct the **Master's in Medical Informatics (MIM)** at FMUP.

## Teaching materials

Most of the practical material I teach from is published here as open, re-usable notes and essays:

- [A practical guide to scientific writing for early-career researchers](/notes/sw/): the full handbook behind *Scientific Communication & Publication* and *Research & Scientific Communication*.
- [Evaluation methods in health informatics](/notes/emhi/): the reading list behind the *Medical Informatics Evaluation* course.
- [Implementing AI in health](/essays/ai-health/): the philosophical frame I open the AI-in-health modules with.
- [Computing Foundations for Health Researchers](/projects/computing-foundations/): the structured learning path I point PhD students at, covering terminal, Git, GitHub, reference management, Markdown writing, and AI-assisted work, aligned with *Productivity Tools* and *Health Data Science Lab Project*.
- [OpenCode: an AI research assistant in your terminal](/notes/opencode/): the hands-on piece I run as a live session for doctoral students.

## What I look for in students

I care less about students being able to define the right jargon and more about whether they can do useful things: evaluate a model, challenge a claim, design a better workflow, write a methods section that does not hide the hard parts, or produce a reproducible analysis that someone else can actually re-run.

## Supervision and collaboration

I welcome PhD co-supervision and Master's thesis supervision in respiratory informatics, clinical decision support systems, mHealth, and evaluation of AI in healthcare. I am also open to external examination and thesis committee participation. For any of the above, the fastest way to reach me is by [email](mailto:tiagojacinto@med.up.pt).
