The Uppsala-Porto collaboration spans more than a decade of joint work on exhaled nitric oxide (FeNO), asthma phenotyping, and type-2 airway inflammation. This bilateral meeting brings together investigators from both sites to review shared datasets, align on methods, and plan next steps.

The talk covers two connected bodies of work. The first is the development and validation of sex-, age-, and height-adjusted FeNO reference equations, culminating in the Global Lung Function Initiative (GLI) FeNO reference values co-authored with the ERS task force and published as a Technical Standard in 2024. Reference values are not a neutral technical product: the choice of reference population, the regression approach, and the definition of the "normal" range all carry clinical weight — they determine how many patients are flagged as having abnormal airway inflammation, and which ones. The talk works through those choices and their implications.

The second body of work is the FeNO Interpretation Aid (FIA), a web tool that embeds the reference equations into a clinical decision support interface. The architecture of the tool — individualised interpretation by sex, age, and height; context-sensitive thresholds; integration with clinical guidelines — is described alongside the practical evaluation that preceded deployment. The talk closes with the current state of FIA 2.0 development, being built in collaboration with NIOX Group plc to extend the tool's reach and incorporate updated reference data.
