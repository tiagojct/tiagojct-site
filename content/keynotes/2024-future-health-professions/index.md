*Universidade do Porto Júnior* brings secondary-school students to the university for hands-on sessions across faculties. This lecture at the Faculty of Medicine introduces the full scope of health professions — not medicine alone, but the allied and informatics-facing roles that sustain and increasingly define how clinical care works.

The lecture frames health careers around a question secondary-school audiences rarely encounter: what does it mean to choose a profession where the tools change faster than any curriculum, and where algorithmic systems are already making recommendations that clinicians accept or override? The answer is not a warning about automation. It is a case for curiosity and critical reading — for developing the habits that allow a professional to evaluate a system, not just operate it.

The first half covers the diversity of health professions: clinical, administrative, informatics, public health, and research roles. The second half narrows to the digital transformation: what AI is actually doing in health today (screening, risk stratification, documentation support), where the evidence base is solid and where it is still thin, and what the EU AI Act means for high-risk clinical applications. Students leave with a more specific picture of health as a field than the generic "doctor or nurse" framing that dominates at this level.
