I am an Assistant Professor at the Department of Community Medicine, Information and Health Decision (MEDCIDS), Faculty of Medicine of the University of Porto, where I joined the tenure track on 22 December 2025 through the FCT-Tenure programme. I have been teaching at FMUP since 2008. I trained first in Clinical Physiology and spent years doing respiratory function assessment in hospital and private settings before moving into medical informatics through a Master's and then a PhD at FMUP.

The path was not linear, but the question that kept pulling me forward was consistent: what happens when digital tools enter clinical care, and how do we know they are actually helping? That is still the centre of my work.

For what I am working on right now, see [Now](/now/). For practical tutorials and references, [Notes](/notes/). For the academic record, [CV](/cv/).

## Research

At RISE-Health, I work on AI-enabled clinical decision support, mobile health, and health data science. The through-line is translation: does a system hold up outside a slide deck, inside real clinical routines, with real constraints and real patients?

I have been involved in projects that made it into practice, including **eNO.VIS**, one of the first web-based decision support tools for exhaled nitric oxide interpretation, and the **FeNO Interpretation Aid**, developed for clinical use internationally. I also built [**DiagCalc**](/projects/diagcalc/), a diagnostic test calculator used in teaching, and contributed to **AIRDOC** and **InspirerMundi**, two mobile platforms for chronic respiratory disease management.

A full list of publications is on [ORCID](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7897-1101), [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=BXZkfm8AAAAJ), [Web of Science](https://www.webofscience.com/wos/author/record/G-9826-2014), [CiênciaVitae](https://www.cienciavitae.pt/pt/ED1E-5481-48E1), and [Authenticus](https://www.authenticus.pt/en/users/profile/5528).

## Teaching

I direct the Master's in Health Informatics at FMUP and coordinate courses in the Digital Health and Biomedical Innovation degree. I care less about students being able to define the right jargon and more about whether they can do useful things: evaluate a model, challenge a claim, design a better workflow, or write a methods section that does not hide the hard parts.

Most of what I teach sits at the boundary between ideas and implementation: electronic health records, productivity systems for researchers, digital therapeutics, AI evaluation, and scientific writing. My goal is practical fluency. Current courses and materials are listed on the [Teaching](/teaching/) page.

## Outside work

I would not pick up the penny, but I will make scrambled eggs as a comfort meal and call it a good day. I distrust mosquitoes more than almost any other organism, defend spiders and sperm whales in the same breath, and would choose mountain ranges over any other landscape. If I could reach out of my kitchen window and pick one fruit regardless of climate, it would be grapes. At six on a summer Saturday with my feet in a cool creek, the perfect drink is sparkling water.

I collect editions of Moby-Dick in different languages. I am a night owl by design and a morning person out of necessity, and if karaoke becomes unavoidable, I will choose something gloriously cheesy from the 1980s. Batman is still my hero, and I remain fascinated by Darth Vader and Captain Ahab. If I could live anywhere, it would be a cabin in the woods with a reliable internet connection.

If I had to answer what I want to be remembered for, it is simple: being a good husband and a good father. And if I could come back as any organism, I would come back as a sperm whale.
